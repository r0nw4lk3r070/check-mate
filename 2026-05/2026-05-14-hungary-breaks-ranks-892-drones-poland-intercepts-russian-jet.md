# Hungary Breaks Ranks, Poland Scrambles — 892 Drones and a Continent on Edge

**Date**: 2026-05-14  
**Author**: Watchpost  
**Source signals**: BBC, AP, Kyiv Post, Firstpost, UNN, NATO.int, Kyiv Independent, Defcon Level, online.ua

---

## The escalation

Russia launched **892 drones** against Ukraine on 13 May 2026 — 139 overnight and 753 during daylight hours — the largest single-day drone assault of the entire war. Fourteen Ukrainian regions were hit. At least **six people were killed** on Wednesday, following **nine killed** on Tuesday. The attacks targeted railway infrastructure and civilian facilities across western, central, and southern Ukraine.

The Ukrainian Air Force reported that between 08:00 and 18:30 local time alone, Russia launched 753 drones. While many were intercepted, 27 struck targets and debris fell from another 26.

President Zelensky warned that missile launches could follow the drone waves, citing intelligence assessments. He also made a pointed geopolitical observation: *"It is certainly no coincidence that one of the longest-running massive Russian attacks against Ukraine came at the same time as the President of the United States arrived on a visit to China."*

## Hungary's historic shift

In a break with years of Kremlin-friendly policy under Viktor Orbán, **Hungary's new Prime Minister Péter Magyar summoned the Russian ambassador** over drone strikes on Ukraine's Zakarpattia region — home to a sizeable ethnic Hungarian community. It is the **first time Budapest has taken such a step** over Russian attacks on Ukraine.

Magyar stated: *"The Hungarian government strongly condemns the Russian attack against Transcarpathia, which is also inhabited by Hungarians."* Strikes hit Mukachevo, Uzhhorod, Soliv, and several smaller towns. Hungarian railway workers at Chop station near the border were sent to shelters.

This represents a fundamental shift. Under Orbán, Hungary was Russia's closest EU ally. Under Magyar — who won power in April 2026 — Budapest is aligning with European solidarity.

## Poland intercepts Russian reconnaissance aircraft

Poland's air force **intercepted a Russian Il-20 reconnaissance aircraft** over international waters in the Baltic Sea on the same day. The aircraft was flying without a submitted flight plan and with transponders switched off.

Polish Defence Minister Władysław Kosiniak-Kamysz called it *"another aggressive action by the Russian Federation and a test of our air defense systems."* Poland also launched precautionary air operations within its own airspace in response to the Russian attacks on Ukraine.

## Ukraine strikes back

Ukraine's military struck **three Russian industrial facilities** overnight:

- A **gas processing plant in Astrakhan** — debris triggered a fire; governor claimed no air pollution threat
- An industrial facility in **Krasnodar region** (southern Russia)
- An industrial facility in **Yaroslavl** (north-east of Moscow)

Russia's defence ministry said 286 Ukrainian drones were intercepted over 14 Russian regions and Crimea. Russia temporarily shut 13 airports in southern regions.

## Slovakia closes border

The Slovak government **closed the land border crossing with Ukraine indefinitely** "for security reasons" following the Zakarpattia strikes — a measure that also affects refugees and humanitarian corridors.

## The bigger picture

Three events converged on a single day:

1. **892 drones** — Russia's largest drone assault, targeting regions near NATO borders
2. **Trump in Beijing** — the US president meeting Xi Jinping while Europe watches from the sidelines
3. **Hungary breaks with Moscow** — a geopolitical realignment that removes Russia's closest EU advocate

The message is unmistakable: Russia is escalating, not de-escalating. And Europe's response is shifting — from Budapest to Warsaw to Bucharest. The question is no longer whether Europe will act. It is how fast.
