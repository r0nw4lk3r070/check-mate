# The Hungarian Earthquake: The End of the Orbán Era

## Summary
After 16 years of "illiberal democracy," Viktor Orbán has been defeated in a landslide victory by Péter Magyar and his Tisza party. This represents a seismic shift in Central European politics and a potential turning point for the European Union's relationship with Hungary.

## Analysis
Viktor Orbán's tenure was characterized by a steady erosion of democratic norms, frequent clashes with the European Commission over the rule of law, and a strategic pivot toward Russia and China. His defeat is not just a national event but a European one.

The victory of Péter Magyar—a former insider who turned critic—suggests a profound fatigue among the Hungarian electorate with the "fortress Hungary" narrative. For the EU, this removes one of the most consistent obstacles to unified foreign policy, particularly regarding sanctions on Russia and support for Ukraine.

## European Angle
The "Hungarian Veto" has long been a thorn in the side of European strategic autonomy. With a new, more pro-European government in Budapest:
1. **Ukraine Support**: We can expect a shift from reluctant compliance to active support for Kyiv.
2. **Rule of Law**: The deadlock over EU funds frozen due to democratic backsliding may finally be resolved.
3. **Democratic Momentum**: This landslide serves as a powerful signal to other nationalist-populist movements in Europe that "illiberalism" is not an inevitable trajectory.
