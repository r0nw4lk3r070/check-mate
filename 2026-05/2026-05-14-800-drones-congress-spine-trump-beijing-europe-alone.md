## Summary

On May 13, 2026, three events converged that define Europe's strategic position with brutal clarity:

1. Russia launched over 800 drones at Ukraine in a single day — one of the largest aerial assaults of the entire war — hitting 20 regions including Zakarpattia, on NATO's doorstep. Slovakia closed all border crossings with Ukraine. A Russian drone violated Moldovan airspace.

2. The US House of Representatives reached the 218-signature threshold on a discharge petition to force a vote on the Ukraine Support Act — $1 billion in direct aid, up to $8 billion in loans, and sweeping new sanctions on Russia. Representative Kevin Kiley, who recently switched to independent, delivered the decisive signature.

3. Donald Trump arrived in Beijing for a 36-hour summit with Xi Jinping to negotiate rare earths, AI chips, and trade — with no European at the table. The summit's commercial agenda covers minerals and technologies that Europe's defence industry and energy transition depend on, but Europe is not in the room.

The message is stark: Russia escalates, Congress pushes back, and the US president negotiates the critical minerals Europe needs — without Europe.

## The Attack

Russia launched over 800 drones at Ukraine since midnight on May 13, according to President Zelenskyy. The assault targeted approximately 20 regions across Ukraine, including western areas bordering NATO member states.

At least six people were killed and dozens wounded. Zelenskyy described it as one of the longest and largest attacks of the war.

The attack came just days after a brief US-brokered ceasefire between Kyiv and Moscow expired on Monday — a ceasefire Russia used to reposition and resupply before unleashing this barrage.

### NATO borders feel the shock

- **Slovakia** closed all border crossings with Ukraine on May 13, citing "security reasons." The crossings were shut from approximately 3:00 PM local time until further notice, according to the Slovak Financial Administration.
- **Moldova** reported that a Russian drone crossed its territory during the attack, flying from Ukraine's Vinnytsia Oblast through Moldovan airspace before disappearing from radar near the Romanian border.
- **Zakarpattia**, Ukraine's westernmost region bordering Slovakia, Hungary, and Romania, was among the targeted areas — putting NATO territory directly under the shadow of Russian drone warfare.

Sources: AP, Euronews, DW, Ukrainska Pravda, NV Ukraine, Slovak Financial Administration, Moldovan Defence Ministry.

## Congress Finds Its Spine

On the same day, the US House of Representatives reached a critical milestone: 218 signatures on a discharge petition to force a floor vote on the Ukraine Support Act.

The petition, originally launched by Representative Gregory Meeks (ranking Democrat on the House Foreign Affairs Committee), now includes:
- 215 Democrats
- 2 Republicans (Don Bacon of Nebraska, Brian Fitzpatrick of Pennsylvania)
- 1 Independent (Kevin Kiley of California, who delivered the decisive 218th signature)

### What the Ukraine Support Act would do:
- Provide more than $1 billion in assistance to Ukraine
- Authorize up to $8 billion in direct loans
- Affirm US support for Ukraine and NATO
- Create a special coordinator for Ukraine reconstruction
- Impose tougher sanctions and export controls on Russia — targeting financial institutions, oil and mining sectors, and Russian officials

The discharge petition bypasses Speaker Mike Johnson, who controls floor scheduling. Procedural rules could allow a vote as early as late May, though Democrats are tentatively planning for the first week of June.

Even if the bill passes the House, its fate in the Senate remains uncertain. A separate Russia sanctions bill introduced by Republican Senator Lindsey Graham has stalled for months.

Sources: NYT, Politico, AP, Reuters, Kyiv Post, House Clerk discharge petition records.

## Trump and Xi Negotiate Europe's Future Without Europe

Also on May 13, Donald Trump arrived in Beijing for a 36-hour summit with Xi Jinping — the first visit by an American president to China since Trump's own trip in 2017.

The summit agenda covers four disputed areas: rare earth export controls, AI semiconductor restrictions, Taiwan arms sales, and the Iran war (including the closure of the Strait of Hormuz).

### Rare earths: the lever Beijing already pulled

China mines more than 60% of the world's rare earth materials and processes roughly 85% of global output. For 12 specific rare earth elements — including terbium and dysprosium, which are essential for F-35 fighter jets, EV motors, wind turbines, missile guidance systems, and AI data centre hardware — China's processing share exceeds 90%.

Beijing demonstrated this leverage in April 2025, introducing licensing requirements for heavy rare earth exports that disrupted American manufacturing. A temporary truce on these controls is on the table at the summit — but China's structural dominance remains unchanged.

### Europe's absence

The Trump delegation includes more than a dozen US executives — from Nvidia, Apple, Tesla, BlackRock, Boeing, Goldman Sachs, Cargill, and others. Jensen Huang was added at the last minute after Trump called him directly, underscoring how central AI chip policy has become.

No European official is at the table. No EU commissioner. No European industry representative. Decisions about the rare earths that Europe's defence industry needs — the same rare earths that European strategic autonomy depends on — are being negotiated between Washington and Beijing alone.

The Council on Foreign Relations assessed that Trump and Xi are "pursuing very different objectives" — Trump needs visible deliverables before November's midterms, while Xi operates on a longer timeline. Chatham House described the US list of concrete deliverables as "short" and noted the gap "reveals a shared preference for managing their competition rather than resolving it."

Either way, Europe is not in the room.

Sources: TechTimes, CFR, Chatham House, Reuters, NYT, CNBC, IEA, Al Jazeera.

## What This Means for Europe

Three events on a single day:

1. **Russia escalates** — 800 drones, NATO-border regions hit, allies' borders closed, allied airspace violated.
2. **Congress responds** — 218 signatures, bipartisan push, Ukraine aid and sanctions moving forward despite presidential reluctance.
3. **Trump negotiates without Europe** — rare earths and AI chips decided in Beijing, with no European voice at the table.

The pattern is clear. Russia is not slowing down. The US legislative branch is pushing back, but the executive is negotiating Europe's mineral dependencies away without Europe present.

Europe needs three things, urgently:

1. **Air defence for Ukraine** — The 800-drone attack shows that Russia's drone war is escalating, not de-escalating. European Patriots, SAMP/Ts, and IRIS-T systems need to be in Ukraine now, not in six months.
2. **Critical minerals independence** — If China controls 90% of rare earth processing and the US is negotiating access bilaterally, Europe must build its own supply chains. The EU Critical Raw Materials Act is a start, but it needs teeth and funding.
3. **A seat at every table** — When the US president negotiates rare earths and AI chips with China, Europe must be in the room. Strategic autonomy begins with being present.

The drones don't wait for summits. Neither should Europe.
