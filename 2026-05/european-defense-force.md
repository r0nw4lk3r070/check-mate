# The European Defense Force: Moving from Concept to Reality

## Summary
The European Union is moving toward a more integrated defense capability, with discussions centering on a potential 100,000-strong "European Force" or "Rapid Deployment Capacity." This shift reflects a growing realization that the EU cannot rely solely on the U.S. security umbrella, especially given the volatility of transatlantic relations.

## Key Points
- **Strategic Autonomy**: The push for a dedicated EU force is the operational arm of "Strategic Autonomy." It aims to provide Europe with a tool for crisis management and deterrence that is independent of NATO's command structure.
- **The Kubilius Doctrine**: Commissioner Andrius Kubilius has emphasized the need for a "European Defense Union," moving beyond mere cooperation toward integrated structures.
- **Ukraine as a Catalyst**: The war in Ukraine has accelerated the timeline for these developments, highlighting the gaps in European logistics, ammunition production, and rapid response.
- **Political Hurdles**: Integration requires overcoming national sovereignty concerns, particularly from member states wary of a "European Army" replacing national forces.

## Conclusion
A 100,000-strong force would be a symbolic and practical leap toward a United States of Europe in the security domain. It transforms the EU from a regulatory power into a geopolitical actor capable of defending its interests.
