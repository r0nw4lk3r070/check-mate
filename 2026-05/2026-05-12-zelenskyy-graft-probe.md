# Zelenskyy Not Implicated in Major Graft Probe — What This Means for Ukraine's Anti-Corruption Fight

**Ukraine's anti-corruption investigators have confirmed that President Volodymyr Zelenskyy is not implicated in a major graft investigation targeting his former chief of staff Andriy Yermak and others. The distinction matters — and Europe should pay attention.**

## What happened

On May 12, 2026, Ukraine's National Anti-Corruption Bureau (NABU) and Specialised Anti-Corruption Prosecutor's Office (SAPO) confirmed that a major corruption investigation into alleged embezzlement and abuse of office does not implicate President Zelenskyy personally. The probe centres on his former chief of staff, Andriy Yermak, and several other officials.

This is significant because Russian and pro-Kremlin disinformation channels immediately seized on the investigation to frame it as "Zelenskyy's corruption" — a deliberate distortion designed to undermine Western support for Ukraine by painting the entire Ukrainian government as irredeemably corrupt.

## What the evidence shows

According to Euronews, Ukraine's anti-corruption investigators explicitly stated that Zelenskyy is not a suspect or person of interest in the case. The investigation targets specific individuals and transactions — not the presidency as an institution.

This is not a cover-up. It is the opposite. Ukraine's anti-corruption institutions — NABU and SAPO — are functioning as independent bodies, investigating powerful figures including those close to the president. That is precisely what a functional rule-of-law system looks like.

The contrast with Russia is instructive. In Russia, anti-corruption investigations are political weapons used selectively against regime opponents. In Ukraine, they are genuine institutional processes that reach into the president's inner circle.

## European context

Three points matter for Europe:

1. **Don't let disinformation weaponise this.** The Kremlin's framing — "Zelenskyy himself is corrupt" — is a known disinformation pattern designed to erode European public support for Ukraine. The facts say otherwise. The investigation is real; the guilt-by-association is manufactured.

2. **Ukraine's institutions are maturing.** The fact that NABU can investigate the president's former chief of staff without political interference is a strong signal that Ukraine's institutional reforms are producing results. This is the same institutional infrastructure that the EU demands as a precondition for accession.

3. **European support must be conditional but not cynical.** Supporting Ukraine does not mean ignoring corruption. It means supporting the institutions that fight corruption — and recognising when they work.

Ukraine is not asking Europe to look away from its problems. It is asking Europe to see the difference between a country that investigates its own officials and a country that murders them.
