# Europe's Empty Chair: The Imperative of European Agency in Ukraine Peace Talks

**As the Kremlin signals a potential end to the war and the US continues to lead peace initiatives, Europe risks being a spectator in the resolution of a conflict on its own soil.**

## What is being said

On May 11, 2026, during a meeting of EU foreign ministers in Brussels, the diplomatic tension surrounding the mediation of the Russia-Ukraine war became evident. Vladimir Putin has suggested that the war may "end soon," while the US remains the primary driver of peace frameworks. Meanwhile, Ukraine's foreign minister, Andrii Sybiha, has explicitly urged the EU to "complement" US peace talks, suggesting that Europe needs to play a more proactive and distinct role in the negotiations.

The Kremlin attempted to inject a specific European voice into the process by suggesting former German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder as a mediator—a move swiftly and decisively rejected by EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas and Kyiv, who viewed it as a cynical attempt by Moscow to dictate the terms of European involvement.

## What the evidence shows

The current diplomatic landscape shows a dangerous asymmetry. While the US manages the high-level strategic dialogue with Moscow, the European Union—the party most affected by the long-term security architecture of the region—is often relegated to a supporting role. 

Kaja Kallas has correctly identified the cynicism in Russia's attempt to use Schroeder as a proxy, noting that allowing Russia to appoint a negotiator on behalf of Europe would be "not very wise" ([The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/live/2026/may/11/ukraine-russia-eu-ministers-brussels-putin-zelenskyy-hantavirus-spain-europe-latest-news-updates)). However, the deeper issue is that Ukraine is now explicitly asking for a European-led diplomatic track to balance the US influence ([Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com/news/ukraine-urges-eu-to-complement-us-peace-talks-with-russia/)). 

The visit of Ukrainian MFA chief Andrii Sybiha to Brussels on May 11 underscores that Kyiv views the EU not just as a source of weapons and funds, but as a necessary diplomatic counterweight to ensure that a peace deal is not simply a "transaction" between Washington and Moscow, but a sustainable European security settlement ([RBC Ukraine](https://newsukraine.rbc.ua/news/ukrainian-mfa-chief-heads-to-brussels-for-1778444683.html)).

## European context

This is the quintessential test of European Strategic Autonomy. If the "peace" in Ukraine is decided solely by the US and Russia, Europe accepts a return to a bipolar world where its security is once again a commodity traded by superpowers.

Strategic autonomy is not just about buying drones or building a joint army; it is about the capacity to lead the diplomatic process that defines the continent's borders and stability. For the EU to "complement" US efforts is not an act of charity toward Kyiv, but an act of self-preservation. A peace process that excludes a primary European leadership role is a process that fails to address the European dimension of the conflict: the long-term stability of the EU's eastern flank and the integrity of the international rule of law.
