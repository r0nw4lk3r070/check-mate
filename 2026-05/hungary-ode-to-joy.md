# The Return of the Anthem: Hungary's Symbolic Pivot

## Summary
Following the swearing-in of Prime Minister Péter Magyar, the Hungarian Parliament performed "Ode to Joy"—the official Anthem of Europe. This symbolic act marks a definitive break from the isolationist and "illiberal" branding of the previous administration and signals Hungary's desire to return to the heart of the European project.

## Analysis
Symbolism in politics is often a proxy for policy. For over a decade, the Hungarian government under Viktor Orbán systematically distanced itself from Brussels, framing the EU as a foreign entity or a "Brussels bureaucracy." 

The public performance of the European Anthem in the halls of parliament is an explicit declaration of identity. It is not merely a musical choice; it is a diplomatic signal. By embracing the "Ode to Joy," the new administration is announcing that Hungary is no longer an "enemy within" the Union, but a partner returning to the fold.

## European Angle
This event is a powerful psychological victory for the European Union. It demonstrates that:
1. **The "Brussels" Brand is Resilient**: Despite years of populist attacks, the core values of the EU (unity, peace, and democratic cooperation) remain attractive and legitimizing for a new Hungarian leadership.
2. **Institutional Re-integration**: The symbolic pivot likely precedes a practical pivot—the unfreezing of EU funds and a shift toward a more cooperative stance on the European Security Architecture.
3. **A Model for Transition**: The transition from an illiberal regime to a pro-European one, punctuated by such a strong symbolic gesture, provides a blueprint for other nations struggling with democratic backsliding.
