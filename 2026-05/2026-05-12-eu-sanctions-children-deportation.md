# EU Sanctions 16 Individuals and 7 Entities Over Forced Deportation of Ukrainian Children

**The European Council has imposed sanctions on 16 individuals and 7 entities responsible for the unlawful deportation and forced transfer of Ukrainian children to Russia. This is the 16th package of sanctions targeting Russia's aggression against Ukraine.**

## What happened

On May 11, 2026, the Council of the European Union adopted a decision imposing restrictive measures on 16 individuals and 7 entities involved in the unlawful deportation and forcible transfer of Ukrainian children to Russia and Russian-occupied territories. The sanctions include asset freezes and travel bans.

This action targets those responsible for the systematic removal of children from their families and communities — a practice that the International Criminal Court has already recognised as a potential war crime, having issued arrest warrants in March 2023 for Vladimir Putin and Maria Lvova-Belova on charges of unlawful deportation of children.

## What the evidence shows

The Council's decision is based on extensive documentation of the forced transfer programme. According to the European Council press release, the sanctioned individuals and entities are "responsible for, implementing, supporting or benefitting from actions that undermine Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity," specifically through the deportation and forced transfer of Ukrainian children.

The UN Commission of Inquiry on Ukraine has previously found that the forced transfers of children constitute war crimes under international humanitarian law. The Yale Humanitarian Research Lab has identified at least 43 facilities across Russia where Ukrainian children have been held, often subjected to "re-education" in Russian language, culture, and patriotic indoctrination.

The European Commission estimates that at least 19,500 Ukrainian children have been deported since February 2022, though the actual number may be significantly higher. Ukraine's government has so far managed to return fewer than 1,000.

## European context

This sanctions package is not merely symbolic. It sends three critical signals:

1. **Accountability**: The EU is naming and sanctioning the individuals and entities directly responsible. This creates a record that will persist beyond the war — these people will face travel bans and asset seizures for as long as the sanctions remain in force.

2. **Deterrence**: Every new sanctions package makes it harder for Russian officials to operate internationally. The cumulative pressure is designed to change the cost-benefit calculation of those participating in the deportation programme.

3. **European moral authority**: At a time when the US is pursuing a separate diplomatic track with Moscow, the EU is demonstrating that it will not compromise on accountability for war crimes — regardless of where peace negotiations lead.

The forced deportation of children is not collateral damage. It is a deliberate policy of erasure — erasing identity, erasing family bonds, erasing the future of a nation. Europe's response must match the gravity of the crime.
