# The High Ground: Space as the New Frontier of European Federalism

**The recent approval of a record €22.1 billion budget for the European Space Agency (ESA) and the "Strategy 2040" is more than a scientific milestone—it is a declaration of strategic independence.**

## What is being said

Space is often framed as a realm of prestige or "pure science"—a place for telescopes and lunar rovers. The public discourse treats space agencies as separate from the "gritty" reality of geopolitical power and terrestrial governance.

## What the evidence shows

The reality is that space is the ultimate "force multiplier" for sovereignty. The evidence from the 2025/2026 ESA Ministerial Council and the "Strategy 2040" shows a decisive shift toward **resilience and connectivity**:

- **The €22.1 Billion Bet**: A record budget is not just for exploration; it is for the "Resilience, Navigation, and Connectivity" initiatives established in early 2026. 
- **Operational Autonomy**: Projects like *Celeste* (LEO-PNT) and the *Argonaut* lunar lander demonstrate that Europe is no longer content to be a passenger on US or Chinese missions. We are building the full-stack capability to launch, land, and communicate independently.
- **EUSPA Evolution**: The transition of the EU Agency for the Space Programme into the *European Union Space Services Agency* signals a move from "managing a program" to "providing a sovereign service" for the entire Union.

## European context

If the EDIP (Defence Industry Programme) is the "nervous system" of European security, the new Space Strategy is the "eyes and ears." 

True strategic autonomy is impossible if the EU relies on foreign constellations for GPS, secure communications, or Earth observation. By integrating space capabilities into the core of the EU's strategic architecture, Europe is effectively claiming the "high ground."

This is federalism by necessity. The scale of space investment is too large for any single member state to sustain. Only a unified European entity can compete in the new space race. By pooling resources at this scale, the EU is not just exploring the moon—it is cementing the structural necessity of a United States of Europe.
