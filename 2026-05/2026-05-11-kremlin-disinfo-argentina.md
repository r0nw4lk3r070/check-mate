# The Global Reach of Kremlin Disinformation: The La Compañía Case

**Russian influence operations are not limited to Europe; the arrest of Dmitry Novikov in Argentina reveals a coordinated effort to destabilize governments across the Atlantic.**

## What is being said
Reports are circulating on Bluesky and via news agencies regarding the detention of Dmitry Novikov, a 26-year-old Russian national in Argentina. He is alleged to be a leader of "La Compañía," an extensive Russian influence network. The network's primary goal was to use "fake news" and disinformation to attack Argentine state institutions and the government of President Javier Milei, specifically targeting those who support Ukraine.

## What the evidence shows
The detention was officially announced by the Argentine government. According to reports from Ukrinform and the Center for Countering Disinformation (CPD), Novikov managed "La Compañía," which operated as a hub for disinformation. 

Multiple sources link this network to deeper Russian intelligence apparatuses. Reports from Nashaniva and United24 Media suggest ties to Russia's Foreign Intelligence Service (SVR) and the Wagner PMC. The operation involved the creation of fake narratives and the use of bot networks to manipulate public opinion in Latin America, mirroring the "Matryoshka" style operations previously seen in Hungary and other EU member states.

## European context
This case is a critical reminder that the Kremlin's "hybrid war" is global. By exporting the same disinformation tactics used to undermine European unity—such as the targeting of pro-Ukraine sentiments—Russia seeks to isolate the West from its partners in the Global South. 

For Europe, this highlights the necessity of the "Security Nervous System" (EDIP and related strategic autonomy initiatives). Disinformation is not just a domestic nuisance but a strategic weapon used to erode the international legitimacy of the EU's support for Ukraine. The ability of a single "curator" to manage a regional network demonstrates the agility and scalability of Russian influence operations, necessitating a coordinated European response that extends beyond its own borders.
