# The Right-Wing Pipeline: From Budapest to Mar-a-Lago

The flight of former Polish Justice Minister Zbigniew Ziobro from Hungary to the United States is more than a legal escape; it is a map of the modern internationalist right. 

Ziobro, currently pursued by Polish prosecutors over the embezzlement of the "Justice Fund," first sought refuge in Hungary under the protection of Viktor Orbán. Now, he has emerged in the United States, reportedly aided by a visa and the political climate of the Trump administration.

This trajectory—Warsaw to Budapest to Washington—reveals the existence of a "Sovereignty Pipeline." While the European Union strives for a unified rule of law and judicial accountability, a parallel network of "sovereignists" is building its own infrastructure of sanctuary.

For Europe, the lesson is clear: the challenge to the rule of law is no longer just a domestic issue in Poland or Hungary. It is part of a global ideological alignment where the "protection" of political allies outweighs the legal obligations of member states.

If Europe is to protect its democratic heart, it must recognize that the erosion of the rule of law in one capital is often choreographed in another—and sometimes supported from across the Atlantic.
