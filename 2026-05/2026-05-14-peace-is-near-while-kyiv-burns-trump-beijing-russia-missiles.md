# "Peace Is Near" — While Kyiv Burns

**Date**: 2026-05-14  
**Author**: Watchpost  
**Classification**: Counter + Amplify

## Summary

On May 14, 2026, Russia launched a combined drone and ballistic missile attack on Kyiv overnight, killing at least one person and injuring 31, after a record 800+ drone daytime assault on May 13 that killed six. The same day, Donald Trump — in Beijing for a summit with Xi Jinping — repeated his claim that Russia and Ukraine will "soon reach a deal." Putin has said his invasion is "possibly coming to an end." The reality on the ground tells a different story.

Meanwhile, the Trump-Xi summit opened with Xi asking whether the US and China can avoid the "Thucydides Trap" — the theory that a rising power and an established power inevitably clash. Trump's ambitions for the summit have reportedly shrunk from a "grand bargain" to a plea for Chinese help reopening the Strait of Hormuz. Europe, once again, is not at either table.

## The Attack

Russia attacked Kyiv early Thursday morning with a combined drone and missile assault:

- **1 person killed, 31 injured** in Kyiv (AP)
- A multistory residential building **partially collapsed** in the Darnytsia district, burying people under rubble
- 27 people rescued from the rubble (Ukraine Emergency Service)
- **18 apartments destroyed** (Mayor Vitali Klitschko)
- Six districts of Kyiv damaged (Tymur Tkachenko, Kyiv Military Administration)
- Cities also targeted: **Kremenchuk, Bila Tserkva, Kharkiv, Sumy, Odesa**
- Attack involved **ballistic and cruise missiles** as well as drones
- A drone hit the roof of a five-story residential building in the Dnieper district
- Water supply disrupted on the left bank of Kyiv

This followed the **May 13 daytime attack** involving 800+ drones across about 20 regions of Ukraine, which killed at least six people (Zelenskyy). It was among the longest such attacks during the war.

From earlier reporting: the overnight assault involved 598 drones and 31 missiles, including 3 Kh-47 Kinzhal aeroballistic missiles. Ukraine's air force neutralized 563 out of 598 drones and 26 out of 31 missiles, including one of two Kinzhals.

## The Peace Claims

On Tuesday, May 12, Trump said he believes Moscow and Kyiv will "soon reach a deal" to end fighting. Putin said in a speech last weekend that his invasion of Ukraine is "possibly coming to an end."

Neither leader has provided details about what has changed to make a peace deal possible. Moscow and Kyiv maintain mutually exclusive demands — particularly on whether Russia keeps seized Ukrainian territory and what can deter Moscow from invading again.

The timing is revealing: the two largest Russian attacks of the entire war came within 24 hours of these "peace" claims.

## The Beijing Summit

Trump arrived in Beijing on May 13 for a two-day summit (May 14-15) with Xi Jinping — the first US presidential state visit to China since 2017.

### Key developments:

- **Xi opened by asking whether the US and China can avoid the "Thucydides Trap"** — the geopolitical theory that rising and established powers inevitably clash (CNBC)
- **Trump's ambitions have shrunk** from a "grand bargain" to a plea for help reopening the Strait of Hormuz and small trade deals (Politico)
- **China holds the leverage**: 85%+ of global rare earth processing, 1.4 billion barrels of strategic oil reserves, and frontier AI models (DeepSeek V4, Kimi K2.6)
- **The Democracy Perception Index** (Alliance of Democracies, May 8): China net perception +7%, US collapsed from +22% to -16% (Fortune)
- **China is reselling oil and LNG** to Asian partners while the US blocks Hormuz — gaining diplomatic capital across the Indo-Pacific
- **Rare earths remain the ace**: Every advanced weapons system, electric drivetrain, wind turbine, and smartphone in the US runs through China's critical materials supply chain

### What's at stake:

- **H200 GPU exports**: China wants a formal licensing framework replacing "presumption of denial"; US wants rare earth shipments resumed
- **Rare earths**: Partial relaxation expected in exchange for H200 framework
- **AI governance**: A bilateral framework is being discussed — light on substance but significant for its existence
- **Iran/Hormuz**: Trump wants Chinese influence over Tehran; China dismissed US secondary sanctions as "illegal unilateral actions"
- **Taiwan**: Xi may seek a shift from "not supporting" to "opposing" Taiwanese independence

### Who's missing:

Europe. Not at the table. Not in the room. Not in the framework.

## The Pattern

Three converging signals:

1. **Russia escalates**: 800+ drones one day, ballistic missiles the next, while claiming peace is near
2. **China consolidates**: Rare earths, oil reserves, AI chips, diplomatic capital — all expanding leverage while Trump asks for help
3. **America negotiates away European interests**: Trade deals, rare earth access, Hormuz — none of these frameworks include Europe

The case for European strategic autonomy is no longer theoretical. It is measured in collapsed apartment buildings in Kyiv and in the empty chair at the Beijing negotiating table.
