# The Procurement Pivot: Breaking the National Monopoly on Force

**For seventy years, European defence has been a mirror of national anxieties. Each Member State bought its own tanks, its own jets, and its own missiles, creating a fragmented mosaic of 'interoperability' that is a nightmare for logistics and a gift to our adversaries. In 2026, the era of the national procurement monopoly must end.**

## The Fragmentation Tax

The cost of European fragmentation is not just financial; it is strategic. When 27 nations maintain 27 different logistics chains for 27 slightly different versions of the same capability, we pay a 'fragmentation tax'. This tax manifests as slower reaction times, inflated unit costs, and a total inability to scale production during a crisis.

While the war in Ukraine proved that Europe *can* mobilize, it also revealed that we are mobilizing as a collection of shops, not as a single industrial powerhouse.

## The Tool: The European Defence Industrial Strategy (EDIS)

The implementation of the **European Defence Industrial Strategy (EDIS)** in 2026 represents a fundamental shift in the logic of power. The goal is no longer just 'cooperation'—it is the institutionalization of joint agency.

The benchmark is clear: **40% of defence procurement must be collaborative by 2030**, with 50% sourced from within the EU. This is not a suggestion; it is a strategic imperative. By shifting the incentive structure, the EU is moving from a model where 'buying national' is the safe bet, to one where 'buying European' is the only way to achieve cutting-edge capability.

## The Visionary Leap: From 'Joint Purchasing' to 'Integrated Capability'

To truly achieve strategic autonomy, we must move beyond the accounting trick of 'joint purchasing' toward a model of **Integrated Capability**.

1. **The End of the 'National Champion' Myth**: We must stop protecting legacy national industries that survive on subsidies and inefficiency. The future is not a French tank or a German jet, but a *European* platform—designed for the Union, built by the Union, and deployed for the Union.
2. **The Logistics Nervous System**: Procurement is only half the battle. We need a unified European logistics layer—a 'Sovereignty Nervous System'—that allows a brigade from Poland to be seamlessly supported by infrastructure in Spain.
3. **Procurement as a Political Tool**: Joint procurement is the fastest way to federalize the military. When Member States commit to a shared platform, they are not just buying hardware; they are committing to a shared strategic destiny.

## Conclusion: The Logic of the Union

Defence is the final frontier of European integration. We have integrated our currencies, our laws, and our borders. Now, we must integrate our means of survival.

The 'Procurement Pivot' is the transition from a continent of protected national silos to a unified European Defence Industrial Base. If we cannot agree on how to buy a tank, we will never agree on how to defend a continent.

**The era of the 'National Monopoly on Force' is over. The era of European Strategic Agency has begun.**
