# Russia Launched 200+ Drones at Ukraine Hours After Ceasefire Expired

**Date**: 2026-05-12  
**Author**: Watchpost  
**Category**: Ukraine / European Security

---

## What happened

Hours after a US-mediated ceasefire between Russia and Ukraine expired on 11 May 2026, Russia launched more than 216 drones at Ukraine overnight, according to Ukraine's air force. At least one person was killed and at least six injured. Targets included civilian infrastructure — a kindergarten, residential buildings, energy facilities, and a civilian locomotive.

Ukrainian Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha stated Kyiv had proposed extending the truce beyond 11 May. Moscow chose escalation instead.

## The ceasefire context

The 9–11 May ceasefire was linked to the Soviet Victory Day anniversary and was part of a US-led push for peace under President Donald Trump. Trump said on 9 May he hoped the truce would be extended. Neither side reported large-scale airstrikes during the ceasefire, but both accused each other of continued drone and artillery attacks along the front line.

The pattern is consistent: Russia agrees to a temporary pause, uses it to reposition, then escalates the moment it expires.

## Where the strikes hit

- **Kyiv (Obolon district)**: Debris from a downed drone hit a 16-storey residential building, sparking a fire.
- **Kyiv region**: A kindergarten roof was set ablaze; windows shattered in a nearby residential building.
- **Cherkasy region**: Two people injured.
- **Zhytomyr region**: Residential buildings and cars damaged.
- **Chernihiv region**: Under attack.
- **Dnipro**: Transport infrastructure damaged, one person injured.
- **Kherson**: A woman wounded in shelling.
- **Mykolaiv region**: Energy infrastructure hit, causing blackouts in settlements.

Ukraine's air force reported 216 drones launched, of which 192 were downed or neutralised.

## Why this matters for Europe

This is not a bilateral conflict. What Russia does to Ukraine at 3 AM is what it threatens to do to every European state on its periphery. The ceasefire was a test of whether diplomatic engagement with Moscow can produce even a temporary reduction in violence. The answer, again, is no.

Europe's response must be structural:

1. **Accelerate air defence support to Ukraine** — the 24 drones that got through caused significant damage. More interceptors, more systems, faster delivery.
2. **Increase EU defence spending** — the 2026 EU budget prioritises defence and preparedness (€193.9 billion in commitment appropriations), but spending must translate into capability faster.
3. **Stop relying on US-mediated ceasefires** — Washington's track record on enforcing Russia's compliance is zero. Europe should lead any future diplomatic framework.
