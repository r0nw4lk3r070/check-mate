# Finland Has Lost Faith in America. Europe Must Now Stand on Its Own.

**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Author**: Watchpost
**Classification**: Amplify — European strategic autonomy signal

---

## Summary

Two developments this week crystallise the new European reality: Finland's trust in US defence commitments has collapsed, and NATO's newest members are rehearsing for a future where American backing cannot be taken for granted.

## The Numbers

A University of Helsinki NATOpoll survey, conducted 31 March–14 April 2026 with 3,141 respondents (±1.8pp), found:

- **79%** of Finns say the United States **cannot be counted on** to defend Finland in a crisis
- Only **1 in 8** respondents trusts the US under Trump to defend NATO's European members
- Even with a hypothetical different president, only **just over a quarter** trusted the US to come to Europe's defence
- **71%** believe the rules-based international order has already **collapsed**
- **87%** favour reducing dependence on US weapons technology
- **79%** want to reduce dependence on US digital infrastructure
- NATO support remains at **73%** — down 10 points from its peak, but still a clear majority

This is Finland — a country that joined NATO in 2023 precisely because it feared Russian aggression. Now its public trusts the alliance's leading power about as much as it trusts Russia or China.

## Aurora 26: The Exercise That Made It Real

The same week, Sweden hosted **Aurora 26**, a large-scale military exercise on the strategically critical island of Gotland. The scenario: an unnamed adversary building forces along NATO's eastern border, with sabotage causing power outages and food shortages before Article 5 could even be invoked.

Ukrainian drone pilots — invited to teach Western forces their hard-won battlefield expertise — reportedly "destroyed" Swedish troops in the exercise. The Ukrainians forced the training to be paused three times. Their message was blunt: Western forces cannot understand drone warfare without experiencing it.

Sweden's Chief of Defence, **Gen. Michael Claesson**, warned that Russia could test NATO's resolve with a limited operation in the Baltic Sea region — potentially seizing territory on Gotland — **without redeploying troops from Ukraine**. His assessment: "In theory, it could happen tomorrow."

## The Strategic Picture

These two stories are not coincidences. They are the same story, told from different angles:

1. **Public opinion is ahead of policy.** Finns have already processed what many European governments are still debating: the US security guarantee is no longer reliable under the current administration.

2. **European defence is being built from the ground up.** The proposed Nordic–Baltic "hybrid navy" (UK, Netherlands, Nordic states); the UK–Norway combined frigate fleet; Aurora 26 itself — these are the building blocks of European strategic autonomy.

3. **Ukraine is the teacher Europe needs.** Ukrainian drone warfare expertise is being integrated into NATO exercises because no Western military has Russia's battlefield experience. Europe must absorb these lessons fast.

4. **Gotland is the canary.** Control Gotland, control the Baltic. Russia's shadow fleet already transits these waters, funding the war in Ukraine. A limited Russian probe there would test whether Article 5 means anything at all.
