# The Iran War and the Fragility of the US Security Umbrella

## Summary
In May 2026, tensions between the United States and its European allies—specifically Italy—have escalated due to the U.S.-led war against Iran. US Secretary of State Marco Rubio's visit to Rome highlights a dangerous trend: the use of security guarantees as a tool for diplomatic blackmail.

## Key Points
- **The Conflict**: The U.S. is engaged in a war with Iran, a conflict that several European nations, including Italy, firmly oppose.
- **The Blackmail**: The Trump administration has shifted from partnership to coercion. The U.S. has already pulled 5,000 personnel from Germany and is threatening further withdrawals from Italy and Spain as "punishment" for their refusal to support the war effort.
- **The Rubio Signal**: Marco Rubio's statement that NATO allies refusing permission to use bases for "projecting force" is "a problem that has to be examined" serves as a veiled threat to the sovereignty of European states.
- **The European Paradox**: While Europe seeks to support Ukraine and maintain stability, it finds itself increasingly vulnerable to the whims of a Washington administration that views allies as vassals rather than partners.

## Conclusion
The current crisis over Iran is a textbook example of why "Strategic Autonomy" is not a theoretical preference but a survival requirement. When the U.S. threatens to withdraw security based on ideological alignment with a specific war, Europe's dependence becomes a liability. A sovereign European defense and foreign policy is the only way to ensure that the continent's security is not subject to the volatility of U.S. domestic politics.
